The path does not remember who walked it last. It holds only the hush of leaves, the weight of mist, and the patient silence of trees that have watched centuries pass without speaking. Here, light does not fall—it seeps, drifting through the canopy like a secret reluctant to be told. Every step sinks into the damp earth as though the forest is gently pulling you deeper, inviting you to listen to what the dark has kept hidden. This is a place where direction loses meaning, where time lingers in the air like breath on cold glass, and where something unseen waits just beyond the edge of sight, neither welcoming nor warning—only watching.
